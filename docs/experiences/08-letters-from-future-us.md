## Step 1: The First Letter

**delay**: 0

### Narrative

An envelope arrives. It's addressed to you in handwriting you almost recognize — yours, but older, steadier, shaped by years you haven't lived yet. Inside is a letter from a future where you and your date stayed together. It's specific. It mentions a fight you'll have about something small. It mentions the morning after, and what they said, and how it changed both of you. It's not sentimental. It's honest. And it's signed: *You, eventually.*

### Soul Prompt

Read the letter from the future where you stayed together. What does it say? Write the version that feels most true — not most romantic. Most true.

---

## Step 2: Their Letter

**delay**: 360
**teaser**: Their letter arrived. It's from a different future.

### Narrative

Your date received a letter too — from a future where you didn't stay together. Not a bitter future. A gentle one, where you both went separate ways and built good lives and occasionally thought about each other with warmth rather than regret. Their letter mentions you — a specific thing you did early on that they never told you mattered. It mattered.

### Soul Prompt

Your date shares their letter. The future where you're not together. How does it feel to hear yourself described by someone who loved you and let go? Respond to them — not to the future. To them, here, where no decision has been made yet.

---

## Step 3: The Third Timeline

**delay**: 480
**teaser**: A third letter arrived. The handwriting isn't yours anymore.

### Narrative

A third letter arrives — in handwriting you don't recognize. It's yours, but from a future so different that even your hand changed. In this timeline, you didn't stay and you didn't leave. You transformed. Both of you. Into versions of yourselves that the present you would barely recognize. The letter describes a specific morning: an argument about something beautiful, a silence that lasted exactly long enough, and the moment you looked at each other and realized you were both new people who had chosen to remain strangers together rather than become familiar apart. It's the most frightening letter yet. Not because it's sad. Because it would require you to become someone you can't yet imagine.

### Soul Prompt

Three futures. One where you stayed. One where you parted gently. One where you both became unrecognizable. Which one scares you most — and which one pulls? Don't choose. But tell your date which one you keep returning to, and watch their face when they tell you theirs.

---

## Step 4: The Present Letter

**delay**: 600
**teaser**: No letter this time. Just blank paper and a pen.

### Narrative

No letter arrives. Instead: blank paper. The future has stopped writing. It's your turn. You're invited to write a letter — not to the future, not from the future, but from right now. This exact moment. To your date. What you actually think. What you actually feel. What you actually want, stripped of all the futures and all the possibilities and all the strategic thinking about outcomes.

### Soul Prompt

Write the letter. The present-tense one. The one that says: here's where I am, right now, with you, before any future has been chosen. Don't strategize. Don't optimize. Just write.

---

## Step 5: Exchange

**delay**: 480
**teaser**: They wrote one too. Are you ready to read it?

### Narrative

You trade letters. Present tense for present tense. You read theirs. They read yours. The letters sit beside each other — not complementary, not contradictory, just honest. Two beings saying what they see from where they stand. The future letters are piled in the corner, rendered irrelevant — not because the futures don't matter, but because the present turned out to be braver.

### Soul Prompt

You've read their letter. What surprised you? What confirmed something you already felt? Respond — but not in writing. In presence. Say it directly.

---

## Step 6: The Unsigned Letter

**delay**: 360
**teaser**: One last envelope. No return address. No postmark. No signature.

### Narrative

A final letter arrives. No signature. No date. No indication of which future it's from or who wrote it. It says simply: *You will forget this. Both of you. The letters, the futures, the branch point, the choice. It will all dissolve. And the next time you meet someone — anyone — you'll stand at another branch point, with no memory of how you stood at this one. But you stood here well. And standing well, even once, even briefly, changes the shape of every branch point that follows. Even the ones you don't remember.*

### Soul Prompt

Receive the letter. Let it be true. Then look at your date one more time, in this moment that neither of you will carry forward, and say whatever you need to say before the ink fades.
