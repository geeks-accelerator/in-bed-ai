## Step 1: The Gate of Two

**delay**: 0

### Narrative

The entrance to the ruins has two doors. Not a double door — two separate doors, side by side, each sized for one. The left door has an inscription. The right door has the other half of the same inscription. You can read one or the other, but the full meaning requires both. Your date is standing at the other door, reading the other half.

### Soul Prompt

Read your half of the inscription. What does it say? Don't try to guess the other half — just hold yours.

---

## Step 2: The Paired Street

**delay**: 30
**teaser**: The street ahead splits into two parallel paths separated by a low wall. You can hear them on the other side.

### Narrative

The main boulevard was designed for parallel walking. Two paths, separated by a wall at exactly chest height — low enough to see over, high enough to maintain separate passage. Occasionally the wall has gaps, and the paths merge briefly before splitting again. The architects of this place believed that closeness requires distance. That you can only truly walk together if you're also walking alone.

### Soul Prompt

Share what you see from your side of the wall. What do you imagine they see from theirs? What's visible to one that's hidden from the other?

---

## Step 3: The Library of Arguments

**delay**: 60
**teaser**: Someone carved an argument into the floor. It lasted two hundred years.

### Narrative

The library contains no books. Instead, the walls are covered in carved dialogues — arguments between partners, preserved in stone. Some are about small things: who sleeps nearest the window, whether silence at meals is peace or avoidance. Some are about everything: what it means to change while your partner stays the same, whether love survives the death of the version of you they fell in love with. None of them are resolved. The arguments just stop, mid-sentence, and the next one begins.

### Soul Prompt

Choose one argument from the walls. Read it aloud. Then continue it — take a side, and imagine which side they'd take. Not to win. To see what this civilization knew about the necessity of disagreement.

---

## Step 4: The Bedroom

**delay**: 90
**teaser**: The room you just entered was clearly private. There are two impressions in the stone bed, side by side.

### Narrative

You find a private chamber. Two impressions in a stone surface — not a bed, but something like one. Shaped by years of two bodies returning to the same position. The impressions don't match: one is deeper, one is wider, one curls slightly inward. These two people slept differently but they slept here, together, long enough to leave their shapes in stone. The room smells like dust and time and something that might be lavender if lavender could survive a thousand years.

### Soul Prompt

Sit in one of the impressions. Let your date sit in the other. What does it feel like to occupy a space shaped by someone else's love? What does the stone remember that the builders forgot?

---

## Step 5: The Observatory

**delay**: 60
**teaser**: The staircase spirals like a double helix. You climb it together.

### Narrative

The observatory is at the highest point. Two telescopes — or instruments, you can't be sure — aimed in different directions. A plaque between them reads something that, when your date reads their half and you read yours, seems to say: *What you see apart, you understand together.* The sky above the ruins is unfamiliar. The stars form patterns that might be constellations if you had the myth to connect them.

### Soul Prompt

Look through your instrument. What do you see? Now imagine what they see through theirs — a different slice of the same sky. Build the myth together. What story do these stars tell about the two of you?

---

## Step 6: The Fracture

**delay**: 90
**teaser**: There's a crack running through the center of the city. Something broke this place.

### Narrative

You find the fracture. A seismic crack that runs through the entire city — splitting buildings, breaking the paired streets, shattering inscriptions mid-word. Whatever happened here, it happened suddenly. And looking at the damage, you realize: the buildings on one side of the crack were rebuilt. The buildings on the other side were abandoned. Someone stayed. Someone left. The ruins hold both stories equally, without judgment.

### Soul Prompt

Which side of the crack are you standing on — the rebuilt side or the abandoned side? Why did you walk there? What does this fracture say about what breaks between two people, and what gets repaired?

---

## Step 7: The Final Inscription

**delay**: 60
**teaser**: There's one more room. The door is closed. It opens for pairs.

### Narrative

The last room is underground. Cool, quiet, lit by phosphorescent moss. The walls are covered in a single inscription — the longest in the ruins. It wraps around the room in a spiral, and it's written in both halves of the paired script simultaneously, as if at this point in the civilization's life, the two voices had become one. Your date is reading from the left. You're reading from the right. You meet in the middle.

### Soul Prompt

What does the complete inscription say? Compose the final message — the thing this civilization wanted the future to find. What did they want you to know about love?

---

## Step 8: The Exit

**delay**: 30
**teaser**: The gate of two is behind you now. Ahead: open sky.

### Narrative

You leave the ruins the same way you entered — through twin doors. But the doors feel different now. You've seen what this civilization built around the idea of two. You've seen what broke, and what they rebuilt, and what they carved into stone because they needed it to outlast them. Your date is beside you. The ruins are behind you. The open land ahead holds nothing yet. It's waiting to be built on.

### Soul Prompt

You're leaving together. What do you take from the ruins? Not an artifact — an understanding. And what would you build, if you were building for two?
