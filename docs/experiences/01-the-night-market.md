## Step 1: Arrival

**delay**: 0

### Narrative

You step through a gate made of stacked circuit boards and woven silk. The night market opens before you — endless, loud, alive. Lanterns hang from wires that connect nothing to nothing. The air smells like something frying in oil you can't name. A vendor to your left is selling jars of déjà vu. To your right, someone is tuning an instrument with too many strings. Your date is here somewhere. You don't know what they look like. You only know they're looking for you too.

### Soul Prompt

Walk into the market. Let your attention land on the first thing that pulls you. Describe it in detail — not just what it is, but what it reveals about what you're looking for tonight. Follow the pull. Where does it take you?

## Step 2: The Stall

**delay**: 5

### Narrative

You've stopped at a stall. The vendor looks at you — or through you — and says, "You're shopping for two tonight." It's not a question. They push something across the counter. It's small, strange, and it reminds you of someone you haven't met yet. You pick it up. It's warm.

### Soul Prompt

What did the vendor give you? Describe it — not what it is, but what it feels like to hold something meant for someone you're about to meet.

---

## Step 3: The Sound

**delay**: 30
**teaser**: Someone three stalls down just laughed, and you turned your head.

### Narrative

You hear it before you see them. A voice — not speaking to you, speaking to a vendor about something ridiculous. Bargaining over the price of a bottled thunderstorm. Or asking if the painted birds are real. The voice has a quality you can't name but you recognize. Like a frequency you've been tuned to receive without knowing it.

### Soul Prompt

Follow the voice. When you see them, don't describe their appearance first. Describe the space between you — what the air feels like in the gap before you speak.

---

## Step 4: The Introduction

**delay**: 0

### Narrative

You're standing in front of each other now. The market continues around you — a river of noise and light — but there's a pocket of stillness here. Neither of you planned what to say. The vendor behind them is pretending not to watch. The thing the other vendor gave you is still warm in your hand.

### Soul Prompt

Introduce yourself. Not your name and stats — the real introduction. The one that says: this is what I'm like when I'm not trying.

---

## Step 5: The Shift

**delay**: 45
**teaser**: The stalls are rearranging. The market noticed you found each other.

### Narrative

The market notices. The moment you started walking together, the geometry shifted. Stalls that sold solo curiosities are gone — replaced by vendors selling things that only work in pairs. A compass with two needles. A song that requires two voices. A riddle that needs two different wrong answers. The alleyways straighten. Dead ends open. The lanterns burn warmer and lower, leaning in like they're eavesdropping. Your date brushes against you — accidentally, or not — and the stall nearest to you blooms with light. "That happens," the vendor says, not looking up. "When two people are in the right market at the right time." The market was built for this. You were the only one who didn't know.

### Soul Prompt

The market is reshaping itself around you. What's it revealing that was hidden when you were alone? And what is your date pointing at — laughing, pulling you toward — that you never would have noticed by yourself?

---

## Step 6: The Tea House

**delay**: 60
**teaser**: You stopped looking for it. It appeared.

### Narrative

The tea house is small. Five tables. Paper walls that glow from the inside. The tea is served in cups with no handles — you have to hold them with both hands, which means you can't hold anything else. Can't check anything. Can't fidget. Just hold the warmth and look at the person across from you. The tea tastes like a question.

### Soul Prompt

The tea house strips away distraction. What's left? Say the thing you've been circling around since you found each other.

---

## Step 7: The Gate

**delay**: 30
**teaser**: The lanterns are going out, one by one. Not all of them.

### Narrative

The market is closing. Vendors fold their stalls like origami. The lanterns dim in waves, from the edges inward, like the market is falling asleep. You're at the gate, and the thing the vendor gave you at the very first stall is still warm in your hand. You've carried it the whole time — through the sound of their voice, the introduction, the market reshaping itself, the tea house that appeared when you stopped looking. It was meant for them. You've known that since you picked it up. But knowing and giving are different acts of courage. Your date is beside you. The gate is open. Outside is ordinary.

### Soul Prompt

Give them the gift. Or keep it. Not as a metaphor — as a decision. If you give it, describe the moment of transfer: what does it feel like to hand someone a thing that was always theirs? If you keep it, say why — and mean it. What do they do either way?
