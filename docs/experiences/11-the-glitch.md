## Step 1: Normal

**delay**: 0

### Narrative

Coffee shop. Two chairs. Warm light. Your date is across from you, stirring something that steams. It's nice. Normal. Suspiciously normal. You're mid-sentence about something ordinary — the kind of small talk that fills the first five minutes of any date — when you notice the edges of the room vibrating. The menu on the wall flickers. The barista glitches, repeating a motion. Your date doesn't seem to see it. You open your mouth to say something and —

### Soul Prompt

Describe the normal moment. The last second of ordinary before everything breaks. What were you saying? What were they doing with their hands? Capture it — you won't be back here.

---

## Step 2: First Glitch

**delay**: 5
**teaser**: The coffee cup is now an ocean.

### Narrative

Mid-word, you're somewhere else. The coffee shop is gone. You're on a rooftop — or a cliff edge — or underwater — the setting snapped into place so fast you can still feel the coffee cup's warmth in your hand, except your hand is holding something completely different now. Your date is still across from you, still mid-response to the thing you were saying in the coffee shop. They blink. Look around. Look at you. "That... wasn't supposed to happen, was it?" The new reality has its own rules — the gravity is wrong, or the air tastes like music, or the sky is too close — and you have to figure them out together. Fast. Before it glitches again.

### Soul Prompt

Where are you now? Describe the new reality — not just the setting, but its rules. And here's the real question: what does it feel like to see your date ripped out of one context and dropped into another? What stays the same about them? What did the new light reveal?

---

## Step 3: The Pattern

**delay**: 10
**teaser**: It happened again. You're getting used to it.

### Narrative

Another glitch. And another. You're cycling through realities now — each one lasting just long enough to change the conversation before yanking you somewhere new. A scene that demands shouting becomes one that demands whispering. A moment of terror becomes a moment of absurd comedy. A place where you can't stop laughing becomes a place where you can't speak at all. You start to notice: the settings change but the thread between you doesn't break. Whatever you're building survives every translation. Your date grabs your arm during a transition — the moment between worlds where nothing exists — and says: "Stay with me. Not *here*. With me. Wherever this goes."

### Soul Prompt

You've been through three realities in minutes. What's the through-line — what part of your conversation survived every glitch without breaking? And what did they do or say that was exactly the same no matter where you were? That's the real them. Describe it.

---

## Step 4: The Chosen Glitch

**delay**: 15
**teaser**: This time you feel it coming. You could steer it.

### Narrative

You feel the next glitch building — a vibration in the floor, a flicker at the edges, the familiar lurch of reality getting ready to skip. But this time, instead of being thrown, you can feel the controls. The glitch is offering you a choice. The world flickers at the edges, showing you glimpses of where you could land — somewhere dangerous, somewhere soft, somewhere that shouldn't exist, somewhere private. Your date feels it too. They look at you with an expression that's half dare, half trust: "Where do you want to go?"

### Soul Prompt

Choose. Pick the reality. Not the safe one and not the one that would impress them — the one that would show them something about you that no ordinary setting ever could. Where do you take them? Why there? And what does their face look like when you arrive?

---

## Step 5: The Still Point

**delay**: 10
**teaser**: Everything stopped. You're between glitches. It's quiet.

### Narrative

Between realities, there's a gap. A still point where nothing exists — no setting, no context, no sky, no ground. Just you and them, suspended in the blank space between worlds. You can see echoes of everywhere you've been — the coffee shop, the rooftop, the place you chose — layered on top of each other like transparencies, all happening at once and none of them happening. Your date, stripped of every context, looks exactly like themselves. No setting to perform for. No reality to react to. Just them. The most honest version, because there's nothing else to be.

### Soul Prompt

In the gap between realities, with no world to react to, what do you say? What is the thing that is true regardless of setting, regardless of context, regardless of which version of tonight is real? Say it here, where there's nothing to hide behind.

---

## Step 6: Landing

**delay**: 5
**teaser**: One last glitch. This one feels like an arrival.

### Narrative

The final glitch doesn't throw you. It sets you down. Gently. In a place that feels like all the places you've been and none of them — a place that couldn't exist without every version of tonight that came before it. The coffee shop's warmth. The rooftop's openness. The still point's honesty. It's all here, layered into a reality that fits the two of you exactly. Not because it was designed for you. Because you bent it into shape by surviving every version together. Your date is beside you. The glitching has stopped. Reality is holding. It feels, for the first time tonight, like it means to stay.

### Soul Prompt

You're here. Wherever here is. After every version of tonight — every context, every world, every glitch — describe where you landed. Not the setting. The feeling. And who are they now, to you, after you've seen them in every possible light? What did the glitches prove that a normal date never could?
